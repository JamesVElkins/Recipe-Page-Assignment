Nigerian Jollof Rice

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a beloved West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, and spices. It's perfect for family gatherings, parties, or Sunday lunch. known for it's rich flavour and vibrant color, this dish is a must-try for anyone exploring Nigerian cuisine.

Recipe Information

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 40 minutes

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of long-grain parboiled rice
  • 5 large tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 large onion
  • 3 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 cups of chicken stock
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons thyme
  • Salt and pepper to tatse

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, and half the onion into a smooth paste.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil and saut√© the remaining chopped onion until fragrant.
  3. Add the blended mixture and tomato paste, then cook for about 10 minutes to reduce.
  4. Stir in curry powder, thyme, salt, and pepper. Add chicken stockand bring to a boil.
  5. Wash the rice throughly, then add it to the pot, stir, reduce heat, and cover.
  6. Cook on low heat for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until rice is tender and fully coated in sauce.
  7. Fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot.

Tips

For extra flavour, use smoked fish or grilled chicken as toppings. You can also add bay leaves while cooking for a richer aroma.
